现在还不是选举日，但是数以千万计的美国人已经经历了排队等候投票的过程。即使对于那些有邮寄和投递选票选择的人，早期投票线在多个州已经延伸。 从理论上讲，提前投票意味着更少的人会试图在选举日参加投票——这是大流行期间的一件好事。但是，当提前投票本身导致大量人群——选民们只是不断来——我们想知道，我们是否真的避免了像我们所希望的那样多的病毒传播。

It's not yet election day, but tens of millions of Americans have already been through the process of waiting in line to vote. Even for those who have the option of mailing and dropping off a ballot, early voting lines have been extended in several states. In theory, early voting means that fewer people will try to vote on Election Day - a good thing during a pandemic. But when early voting itself leads to large crowds - and voters just keep coming - we wonder if we've really avoided spreading the virus as much as we'd hoped.
